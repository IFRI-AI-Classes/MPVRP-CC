Introduction

The distribution of multiple products across logistics networks represents a major economic and environmental challenge in industries such as petroleum, chemicals, and food supply. Beyond transportation expenses, logistics operators must also account for operational costs related to fleet maintenance and product management between consecutive distribution rounds. These additional costs significantly influence planning decisions but are rarely considered in classical vehicle routing formulations.

We introduce the Multi-Product Vehicle Routing Problem with Changeover Costs (MPVRP-CC), a new variant of the Vehicle Routing Problem designed to incorporate these operational constraints into multi-product distribution planning. In particular, the model captures the cost incurred when a vehicle changes the product it carries between successive tours, reflecting cleaning, maintenance, or reconfiguration operations required before transporting another product.

By explicitly integrating changeover costs into routing decisions, the MPVRP-CC provides a more realistic framework for optimizing distribution operations in sectors where vehicles handle multiple product types. The objective is to minimize the overall distribution cost while ensuring efficient deliveries from depots to customers under practical operational constraints.
